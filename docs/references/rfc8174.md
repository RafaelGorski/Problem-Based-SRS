# RFC 8174 — Ambiguity of Uppercase vs Lowercase in RFC 2119 Key Words

> **Reference collected from:** <https://www.rfc-editor.org/rfc/rfc8174>  
> **Collected (UTC):** 2026-04-29T14:37:00Z  
> **Updates:** [RFC 2119](./rfc2119.md)

## Summary

RFC 8174 (BCP 14), authored by Barry Leiba (Huawei Technologies, May 2017), clarifies RFC 2119 by establishing that key words have their defined special meanings **only when they appear in ALL CAPITALS**. When not capitalized, they have their normal English meanings.

## Key Clarification

> "The words have the meanings specified herein only when they are in all capitals."
>
> "When these words are not capitalized, they have their normal English meanings and are not affected by this document."

This resolves the ambiguity in RFC 2119 where "often capitalized" left room for interpretation of lowercase instances.

## Updated Boilerplate

RFC 8174 updates the recommended boilerplate to:

> The key words "MUST", "MUST NOT", "REQUIRED", "SHALL", "SHALL NOT", "SHOULD", "SHOULD NOT", "RECOMMENDED", "NOT RECOMMENDED", "MAY", and "OPTIONAL" in this document are to be interpreted as described in BCP 14 [RFC 2119] [RFC 8174] when, and only when, they appear in all capitals, as shown here.
